Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about blood donation.

Who can donate blood?

Most people in good health, at least 17 years old (16 with parental consent in some states), and weighing at least 110 pounds can donate. Specific eligibility criteria apply.

How often can I donate?

You can donate whole blood every 56 days. Platelet donors can give more frequently - up to 24 times a year.

Does it hurt?

You may feel a brief pinch when the needle is inserted, but most donors report feeling only minor discomfort during the donation process.

How long does it take?

The entire process takes about an hour, with the actual donation taking only 8-10 minutes.
